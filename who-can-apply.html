Who can apply

Eligible agencies can apply for the Children’s special allowances (CSA) for each child they maintain.

Eligibility criteria for CSA

To apply for the CSA, your agency must meet the following conditions:

  • Your agency maintains a child that:
    • is under the age of 18,
    • physically resides in Canada,
    • depends on the agency for care, residence, maintenance, education, training and advancement to a greater extent than on any other agency.
  • Your agency must be one of the following:
    • Government-Run or Appointed Organization
      • a department or agency of the federal, provincial, or territorial government
      • an agency appointed by a province, territory, or its authorized authority to administer child protection laws
    • o Indigenous Governing Body
      • an Indigenous governing body or its department or agency
      • an agency appointed by the Indigenous governing body or its authorized authority to administer child protection laws
    • o Licensed or Authorized Institution
      • any institution legally licensed or authorized by the province or territory to provide custody or care for children
      • this includes foster homes, group homes, and guardianship arrangements established under a court order
